The Crisis

More than half of all adolescents will struggle with a serious mental health issue.

The numbers are staggering. The stigma is real. And too often, the silence is fatal. We're changing that — one honest conversation at a time, in classrooms, gymnasiums, and community spaces across the country.

Our Origin

It's my son. Your daughter. Their best friend.

"
We had one thing in common — we all knew someone who was struggling with addiction and overdose. We were terrified we could be next.
— A Generation S.O.S. Founder

Generation S.O.S. was born several years ago in New York City. Six high school students died of accidental overdoses within just a few weeks. The schools' lack of response was shocking — a painful example of the shame and stigma surrounding addiction.

A group of high school students met to talk honestly about the drug epidemic devastating their world. They urgently needed help navigating this crisis. Society had failed them. So they turned to each other and started the conversation.

Today, that conversation has grown into a national movement — reaching teens in middle schools, high schools, colleges, places of worship, and community organizations across the country.
